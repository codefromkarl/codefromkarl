## What I Explore

Putting AI into the thinking, learning, coding, writing, and feedback loop, rather than treating it as an external assistant.

When models can draft, code, summarize, and imitate, the human problem shifts from "can this be produced?" to "what deserves attention, judgment, and responsibility?".

Continuously exploring the boundaries of AI:

1. **Attention and agency**: what deserves sustained human focus when machines can generate answers instantly.
2. **Human-AI collaboration**: how to make AI-assisted work inspectable, recoverable, and easier to reason about.
3. **Learning in public**: turning projects, notes, and experiments into artifacts that show how thinking changes over time.
4. **Practical tools**: building infrastructure that makes better AI workflows possible without hiding human decisions.

## Selected Projects

### [ContextAtlas](https://github.com/codefromkarl/ContextAtlas)

An experiment in making AI collaboration more context-aware. It focuses on retrieval, project memory, code understanding, and observability so AI tools can work with a clearer picture of the project instead of guessing from a thin prompt.

### [meta-harness](https://github.com/codefromkarl/meta-harness)

An experiment in treating AI workflow improvement as evidence, not vibes. It turns candidates, runs, scores, benchmarks, proposals, and traces into artifacts that can be replayed and compared.

### [TrendRadar-Rust](https://github.com/codefromkarl/TrendRadar-Rust)

A Rust-based trend monitoring and RSS aggregation engine that turns scattered hot lists, feeds, and news sources into a fetchable, filterable, rankable, storable, and reportable information radar. For me, it explores how tools can protect human attention in an age where AI makes content production cheaper and noisier. A personal attempt to learn Rust.

## Principles

- Attention is the scarce resource. AI should help protect it, not consume more of it.
- Automation should increase human agency, not turn people into passive reviewers of machine output.
- Tools should make judgment easier to exercise and easier to audit.
- Good engineering matters because values need reliable systems to survive contact with reality.
